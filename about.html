Bio

I’ve most recently been reading and writing about decentralization.

I currently serve on the board of SAEP-USA, and I hold a degree in Economics and Design from UNC Chapel Hill.

I’m currently based in the beautiful borough of Brooklyn, NY.

Interests

I have a strong appreciation for nature, technology, and economics. I find myself most interested in projects that bring these topics together, such as urban agriculture, smart contracts, prediction markets, and micromobility.

Dozens of other solutions addressing climate change, economic inequality, and inefficient government continuously pique my interests.

My interest in data, economics, and design all tie back to a core curiosity about decision making. How can we design systems so that the easiest decision is also optimal?

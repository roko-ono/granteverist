Welcome! I use this site to share past and present interests, ideas, and projects. Hope you enjoy taking a look around!

Bio

My background is in data science and project management, which I’ve most recently been applying to managing Caleb Rudow’s inaugural campaign for NC House District 116 (update!!! we won!).

I currently serve on the board of SAEP-USA, and I hold a degree in Economics and Design from UNC Chapel Hill. I’m currently based in my hometown of Asheville, NC.

Interests

I have a strong appreciation for nature, technology, and economics. I find myself most interested in projects that bring these topics together: urban agriculture combines machine learning and robotics with sustainable agriculture, smart contracts combine mechanism design and governance structure with blockchain immutability, and prediction markets combine financial market design with politics and current events. Dozens of other solutions to climate change, economic inequality, and inefficient government continuously pique my interests.

I contribute to projects like these by using data science to inform efficient and accurate decision making. My experience in data, economics, and design all tie back to a core curiosity about decision making, and this has led me to data science. “How can we design systems so that the easiest decision is also optimal?” must be predicated by answering “what outcomes are optimal?”

We all have ideas of what optimal looks like, but there’s always truth in numbers. Finding that truth is the artful side of data science that I enjoy most.
